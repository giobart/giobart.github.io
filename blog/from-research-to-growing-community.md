# Oakestra: From Research Idea to a Growing Community

**Dec 2025** | *Giovanni Bartolomeo*

---

Oakestra (https://oakestra.io) officially started in 2021 as a research idea proposing a new approach to Edge Service Orchestration. It was published as a research paper in 2023 at [USENIX ATC](https://www.usenix.org/conference/atc23/presentation/bartolomeo), and has continued growing ever since.

Today, Oakestra is not only a research effort and an [open-source project](https://github.com/oakestra/oakestra), but it is also growing strong as a community. These passionate people are truly shifting this project from research to reality.

I'm proud of the passion, respect, and insights shared weekly during our maintainers' meetings, and daily in our issues and PRs. This project is not just a canvas of ideas for edge computing, but it has grown into a place where everyone feels empowered and free to share their opinions. I believe we should not take this for granted.

Therefore, I want to heartily thank all the incredible contributors of this project; you're truly amazing! 🤩

### Our very fist community award 🥇

This year, for the very first time, with our community we wanted to celebrate someone who has distinguished themselves within an already amazing team of people. In particular, we want to show a small token of appreciation to a person who has substantially impacted the community with their ideas.

#### Thank you Alexander Malyuk for your incredible contribution to the Oakestra Community 🎉

![/blog/img/1766400889265.png](/blog/img/1766400889265.png)

We decided to give this recognition to [Alexander Malyuk](https://github.com/Malyuk-A) for his incredible contributions and his continuous effort in providing valuable insights and suggestions. 

Alexander is the main contributor behind [Federated Learning Operations (FLOps) in Oakestra](https://www.oakestra.io/docs/manuals/flops-addon/flops-manuals-overview/). He also radically changed the way weekly community meetings are organized, and brought countless quality of life improvements to our group.

![/blog/img/1766400875089.png](/blog/img/1766400875089.png)

Alex also quickly became our Notion Guru. He organized a space for everyone to keep track of tasks, take notes, share ideas, ask questions and discuss issues. Do you like the way this Notion page is organized? That's all thanks to him! 👏

> "My request to every single one of you is to pick just one person — someone you know, a contributor to an OSS project you like, or an indie artist you enjoy — and simply write to them or give them a call. Let them know they are appreciated." - **Alexander Malyuk**

Let’s treasure these sentiments, and in the spirit of the holidays, let's take a moment to recognize the people around us.

Thanks to all the amazing people involved in this project!
